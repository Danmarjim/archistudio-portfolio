How many times have I heard, mid-project: "we'll sort out the colours at the end"?

Colour is not an accessory you add once everything else is in place. It is one of the founding elements of a project — just as much as the layout of spaces or the choice of materials.

A wall painted blue is not simply a blue wall. It is a wall that makes a room feel smaller or larger, that absorbs or reflects light, that creates tension or ease, that says something about the people who live there.

In the Loving Colors project we built colour palettes from the natural light in each room, the orientation of the flat, and above all the personality of our clients. The result is a space that breathes, that moves you, that is unmistakably theirs.

That is the power of colour used with intention: not to beautify a space, but to define it.
