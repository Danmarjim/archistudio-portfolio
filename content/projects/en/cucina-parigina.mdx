A complete restyling of a 1980s kitchen — outdated and tired — which we swept through with a breath of fresh air.

The key feature is the "swimming pool" effect, achieved by using floor tiles to clad the walls halfway up, protecting them while adding depth and character.

The square insert pattern and the bistrot table with wrought iron legs take us straight to the narrow streets of Paris, lending the whole space a quietly romantic feel.

The colour palette is neutral — for a style that is both luminous and timeless.
